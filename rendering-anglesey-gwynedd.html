Silicone render that stays clean and flexible

Silicone systems give a modern textured finish that resists algae and hairline cracking. Ideal for coastal properties around Anglesey, Gwynedd and North Wales, silicone render lets walls breathe while keeping rain out.

  • Mesh reinforced basecoat for stability
  • Choice of grain size and hundreds of colours
  • Low maintenance, water repellent surface
  • Great for old and new substrates including blockwork and insulation boards

Installation steps

  • Prep - remove loose material and prime.
  • Base - apply adhesive base with full mesh embed.
  • Key - levelling passes for a flat plane.
  • Finish - silicone topcoat in the selected colour and grain.
Can you colour match an existing elevation?
Yes. We can match close equivalents or repaint adjoining areas for a uniform appearance.

Monocouche through colour render for crisp lines

Monocouche is a one coat, through colour render that is machine sprayable and scraped back for a sharp, uniform look. It delivers strong weather protection and fast application that keeps projects on schedule.

  • Through colour means no painting required
  • Scraped finish with crisp arrises around windows
  • Large colour palette with UV stable pigments
  • Excellent value for new builds and extensions
Fast application Through colour Uniform finish
Is monocouche suitable for old stone walls?
It can be, but we assess breathability and movement first. Silicone systems are often better on mixed or moving substrates.

Traditional sand and cement rendering with modern additives

For cottages, farmhouses and repairs to existing areas, a well mixed sand and cement system still performs brilliantly. We use correct ratios, waterproofers and fibres, followed by a float or sponge finish ready for paint.

  • Patch repairs and full elevations
  • Chimney and weather detail expertise
  • Breathable paints and colour advice
  • Tie in neatly to stone features and sills

Working across Bangor, Caernarfon, Holyhead, Menai Bridge, Llandudno and Conwy, we protect your home from wind driven rain and lift kerb appeal.

How long before we can paint new render?
Sand and cement needs a longer cure. We will advise safe paint windows based on weather and thickness.
